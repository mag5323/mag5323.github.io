Mining overlapping and hierarchical communities in complex networks

Definition

1. Overlapping

several nodes in the network may belong to multiple communities.

example

individuals have families, co-authors and friends.

2. hierarchical organization

several small communities are incorporated into larger ones in turn until they both form a whole network; and such a bigger community can afterward be split into several smaller communities.

example

several individuals make up a family; thousands of families incorporate into a city; then hundreds of cities
group into a state; and finally several states make up a country.

Motivation

A common framework that can extract both overlapping and hierarchical organization.

  1. Extract all meaningful units which can be maximal sub-graphs [1], maximal cliques [3], or even a sole node [4] from the
    original social network then each meaningful unit is taken as an initial community.
  2. Adopting a specified similarity measurement repeatedly incorporate two communities with maximal similarity into a
    new, larger one until only one community remains, which is a dendrogram.
  3. Cut through the dendrogram generated in step (2) to obtain an integral community organization represented in the form
    of overlapping and hierarchical structures, in which quality reaches, or nearly reaches a local or global optimum.
